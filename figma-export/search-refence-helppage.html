Specific Keyword Operators: Feed The likeCount and bookmarkCount keywords allow you to search for posts that has likes and/or bookmarks equal to, less than, or greater than the specified number. To do so, specify the number after adding any of the following symbols: = &gt; &lt; &lt;= &gt;= Ex: likeCount&gt;=30 searches for all posts with 30 or more likes Events You can specify who is hosting an event by including the host: keyword by including their username after the colon Logical Operators All of these operators and syntax can be combined with brackets, the OR operator ||, and the AND operator & to adjust your search. Ex: order:date_asc (-ball || -hat) & likeCount&gt;100 searches for all posts that do n’t include the keywords ball or hat, have more than 100 likes, and will sort the posts with the oldest date first Additionally, the same logical operators and brackets can be applied to both hashtags and already inputted tags to help specify your search Ex: (Beginner || Bench Shooting) will search for events that either have the beginner skill tag or the bench shooting sport tag

Basic Keyword Syntax: Quotation Marks search for an exact phrase or word Ex: “Plains” searches for all things that contain the word Plains with the specific capitalization given. The minus symbol searches for things without that specific keyword Ex: -ball searches for all things without the keyword “ball” Sorting: You can use the order: keyword to sort results ahead of time.
